Privacy Policy for Callout Fault-Tec Pip-Alarm

Last updated: December 18, 2024

Dan Forsberg ("I", "my", or "me") develops and maintains the Callout Fault-Tec Pip-Alarm mobile application (the "App"). This Privacy Policy explains how information is collected, used, and protected when you use the App.

Open Source

The App and its backend infrastructure are open source. You can review the source code, including the AWS infrastructure-as-code (IaC), at: https://github.com/dforsber/callout-pip-alert

Self-Hosted Architecture

The App is designed to connect to self-hosted backend infrastructure that you deploy and control in your own AWS account. When using a self-hosted backend:

  • All user account data remains in your own AWS infrastructure
  • Authentication is handled by your own AWS Cognito instance
  • I do not have access to your data or credentials
  • You are responsible for the security and privacy of your own deployment

The App allows you to configure your own backend endpoints in the settings, giving you full control over where your data is stored and processed.

Demo Backend

I provide a demo backend for evaluation and testing purposes. If you use the demo backend:

  • Account Information: Your email address is collected for authentication and account verification
  • Authentication Data: Your email and encrypted password are stored in the demo AWS Cognito instance
  • Device Token: Your Apple Push Notification service (APNs) device token is collected and stored to enable push notifications for incident alerts

The demo backend is intended for evaluation only. For production use, deploying your own backend infrastructure is recommended.

How Demo Backend Information Is Used

For users of the demo backend, the information is used to:

  • Create and manage your demo user account
  • Authenticate you when you log in to the App
  • Send account verification and password reset emails
  • Deliver push notifications for incident alerts to your device
  • Provide the demo service functionality

Data Storage and Security

Self-hosted users: Your data is stored in your own AWS infrastructure under your control and security policies.

Demo backend users: Account information and device tokens are stored using AWS services. Appropriate technical and organizational measures are implemented to protect your personal data.

Data Retention

Self-hosted users: Data retention is managed by you in your own infrastructure.

Demo backend users: Account information is retained for as long as your demo account is active. Contact me to request account deletion.

Third-Party Services

The App integrates with the following services:

  • AWS Services: The backend infrastructure (whether self-hosted or the demo backend) uses AWS services including Cognito for authentication. AWS services are subject to the AWS Privacy Policy.
  • Apple Push Notification Service (APNs): To deliver push notifications to your device. This service is operated by Apple Inc.

Your Rights

Self-hosted users: You have full control over your data in your own infrastructure.

Demo backend users: You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data held about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your demo account and associated data
  • Withdraw consent for data processing

Children's Privacy

The App is not intended for children under the age of 13. Personal information is not knowingly collected from children under 13.
